Privacy Policy for Dark Hour

Last Updated: June 4, 2026

Dark Hour is a static, 100% client-side web application designed to help individuals visualize and analyze sleep patterns. This Privacy Policy explicitly describes how Dark Hour accesses, uses, stores, and handles your Google user data.

1. Information We Access (Google User Data)

To provide sleep visualization and circadian analysis, Dark Hour requests access to your sleep data via the Google Health API (or respective Google Health/Health Connect REST endpoints).

Specifically, the app requests permission to read:

  • Your historical and real-time sleep records (e.g., sleep start/end times, sleep duration, and efficiency metrics).
  • Detailed sleep stage data (e.g., Light, Deep, REM, Awake cycles), when available in your Google account.

2. How We Use Your Google User Data

Dark Hour uses your Google user data strictly to power the local interface and functionalities of the app. This includes:

  • Rendering a canvas actogram (one row per day) with distinct sleep stage colorings.
  • Performing periodogram calculations for circadian frequency analysis.
  • Computing a purple "circadian night" overlay based on your visible dataset.
  • Forecasting estimated circadian night trends 2, 7, or 30 days into the future.

The data is loaded progressively and cached entirely within your local browser environment to improve performance on subsequent visits.

3. Data Sharing, Transfer, and Disclosure

Dark Hour does not share, transfer, or disclose your Google user data with any third parties, external servers, advertising platforms, or data brokers. Dark Hour developers also do not access any of your data.

Because Dark Hour is a static Single Page Application (SPA) with no backend server or database framework, your Google user data never leaves your local device. All API requests go directly from your web browser to Google's official endpoint (health.googleapis.com).

We do not use any third-party tracking cookies, analytics frameworks (like Google Analytics), or marketing pixels that could capture or leak your information.

4. Data Storage, Security, and Retention

  • Authentication: Authentication is handled via a secure OAuth 2.0 PKCE flow. Your access and refresh tokens are stored purely in your browser's localStorage.
  • Data Caching: Fetched sleep records are stored locally on your device inside an IndexedDB cache so you can browse data seamlessly.
  • Data Retention: You retain complete control over your data. Clicking "Clear cache" within the app immediately purges all local tokens, cached sleep points, and active variables from your browser storage.

5. Revoking App Access

You can disconnect Dark Hour from your Google Account at any time by revoking its permissions through the official Google Account Permissions Manager.

6. Compliance with Google API User Data Policy

Dark Hour's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs to any other app will adhere to Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

7. Contact and Support

As an open-source tool without corporate infrastructure, any questions regarding this policy or the app's security can be directed to the official project repository by opening an issue at: https://github.com/Aozora7/darkhour.
